ABOUT ME

Hello! I'm Hamza, a PhD student in Organization Studies at the Boston College Caroll School of Management.

My research explores how individuals and organizations address fundamental, self-defining questions. I study this by relying on two main research streams: (1) identify, and (2) purpose.

I am a field researcher and use inductive qualitative research methods to study diverse contexts such as a Jewish Service Organization, members of the occupation of Campus Muslim Chaplains, Surgeons and Anesthesiologists, and Business Schools.

MGMT 102113 | Introduction to Organizational Behavior

This class provides an introduction to organizational behavior, generally defined as “study of both group and individual performance and activity within an organization”. This course covers a wide array of theories and practical applications dealing with topics such as motivation, creativity, negotiations, workplace emotions, decision making, leadership and organizational culture.
